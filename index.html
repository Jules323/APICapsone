Can you imagine a world without tigers, gorillas, or elephants? In the past 500 years, there have been nearly 1,000 species that have gone extinct. Perhaps one of the most striking elements of the present extinction crisis is the fact that 90% of primates, are severely endangered. This group - monkeys, lemurs, orangutans, and apes - live in tropical forests which are fast disappearing. Overall, there are estimates that half the globe's 5,491 known mammal species are declining in population and are at risk of disappearing forever. In addition to primates, marine mammals - including several species of whales, dolphins, and porpoises - are slipping quickly toward extinction.
